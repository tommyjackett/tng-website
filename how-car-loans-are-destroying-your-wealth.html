EP 226

How Car Loans Are Destroying Your Wealth

Nick argues that rolling car loans over every few years quietly erodes long term wealth, using a young couple on 240 thousand with two car loans as a worked example. Clearing the loans lifts their borrowing capacity by 235 thousand, and at 5 percent growth over 30 years that extra home value compounds to about a million dollars. The trio compare paying cash, holding a car for five to ten years, buying a new Toyota with a warranty over an ageing European car, and going in with a bigger deposit. Nick closes on when a car loan is justified, since a 6 percent loan only pays off if your alternative investment beats 6 percent.
