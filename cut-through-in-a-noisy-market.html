EP 190

Cut Through In A Noisy Market

Jason opens with Mr Beast, tracing how Jimmy Donaldson went from 76 YouTube followers as a teenager to more than 151 million subscribers, and uses his Feastables car giveaway and supermarket stunt to show what makes content cut through: consistency, audience engagement and philanthropy. The hosts then turn it on themselves. Marty recalls buying a hundred coffees to meet school-run mums and argues cut-through has to come from your own personality, while warning against pitch with no punch and vanity metrics. Jason stresses that margins must support the marketing spend and that a plan you stick to beats sporadic posting. Nick makes the case for presenting to a live audience, treating podcast, website and Google reviews as connected social proof, and measuring ROI by asking every caller how they heard about you.
