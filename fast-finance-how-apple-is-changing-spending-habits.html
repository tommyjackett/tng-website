EP 192

Fast Finance

How Apple is Changing Spending Habits

Nick brings the rise of Apple and its push into buy now pay later, where iPhone users linked to an ANZ card can tap and choose to pay over a three-month term. The trio worry about what universal, frictionless credit does to basic money management, especially for kids who never grew up with cash. They contrast lay-by and physical cash discipline with instant gratification, unpack why financing a depreciating car makes no sense, and flag how buy now pay later charges fees rather than interest to sidestep regulation. Jason covers Apple's data play, from the digital wallet and Apple Vision fitting rooms to banks paying Apple over 130 million dollars a year, and Marty lands on investing in Apple rather than just being milked by it.
