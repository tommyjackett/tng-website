EP 218

Is Home Ownership Becoming a Fantasy in Australia?

Marty compares buying property in Melbourne in 2000 against 2025 and shows how the share of income needed for a mortgage jumped from about 30 percent to more than 80 percent on one wage. The trio place Australian cities on a global price map, with Sydney at 13.8 times income and Adelaide and Brisbane now in the top ten, and ask how much further prices can stretch before households cap out. They weigh migration, tax reform and falling birth rates, then turn to what buyers can actually do. Practical options cover paying down debt fast, apartment and build to rent living, rentvesting into regional property, subdividing, and upskilling rather than speculating.
