EP 198

The Rise of Private Lending in Australia

Marty opens with signs the rate cycle is turning, a major bank cutting three year fixed investment rates, mortgage arrears near one percent and housing price growth plateauing, and a quick take on whether Australia heads into recession. The main segment explains private credit, the fast growing non bank lending market. Marty and Nick cover how it lets asset rich borrowers with weak cash flow access funds for a business acquisition or property deal, the need for a clear exit strategy, the two to three percent higher rates and larger establishment fees, and why the speed suits time sensitive opportunities. They warn about sharky lenders who profit when a deal defaults, stress dealing with an experienced broker, and note banks are competing back with more flexible commercial lending under two million.
