EP 232

Dollar Cost Averaging - The Easiest Way to Build Wealth Over Time

Nick explains dollar cost averaging, spreading investment into the market over time rather than trying to time the bottom. The trio walk through a 200,000 dollar lump sum split over six months, why superannuation is automatic dollar cost averaging, and how rebalancing takes profit from the winning side and buys the cheaper one. Marty shares research that timing dips only returned 7 to 8 percent against the S and P 500's 13 percent, and that day traders burn 90 percent of their capital in 90 days. Jason adds the tax angles, salary sacrifice, family trusts and the value of automating a set and forget habit.
