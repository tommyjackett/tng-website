EP 227

Rethinking Property Tax in Australia

Marty brings a listener question from Darren McLeod on whether negative gearing should be scaled by property count rather than scrapped. The trio weigh the Parliamentary Budget Office cost of about 165 billion over a decade, and debate whether negative gearing helps the wealthy or mum and dad investors. Nick pitches a simpler model that taxes only the net gain after accumulated shortfalls, and they cover the CGT discount, depreciation clawback on sale, housing supply, and why Australia taxes homes differently from the United States.
