EP 231

Knowing When to Quit in Business

Marty introduces the dead horse strategy, when you keep pouring time and money into an idea, a service line or a person that is not working. Jason recalls a gin venture, Marty describes chasing real estate off the back of his mortgage firm, and Nick applies the lesson to referrer relationships and underperforming staff. They cover sunk cost and ego, setting KPIs and stop-loss style exit points, and leaning on outside advisors. Marty closes on Godfrey's, the vacuum retailer that clung to physical stores, missed robotic vacuums and fell into liquidation owing millions.
