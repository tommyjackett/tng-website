EP 239

Are Low Interest Rates Gone for Good In Australia?

Jason opens by asking when he can buy a house at a low rate, and Nick's answer is blunt: the two to three percent era was a pandemic Black Swan and the new normal sits around five percent. Nick explains the difference between the RBA cash rate and a consumer home loan rate, the neutral or Goldilocks rate near three percent, and why government deficits plus spending on AI and green energy push the neutral rate higher. Marty and Jason connect it to housing, where migration, a skilled labour shortage and build costs keep affordability stretched and prices high. They close on what people can control: run a household budget like a business, use surplus income to pay down debt, review your rate annually, and stop waiting for cheap money to return.
