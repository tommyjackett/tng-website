EP 241

The Retirement Boom That's About To Change Australia

Jason lays out the numbers behind Australia's retirement wave: 140,000 people retiring each year, 4.2 million retirees at an average age of 56.9, and the over-65 population set to grow from 3.2 million to 8.1 million by 2050. The trio work through the economic ripple effects across healthcare, aged care, the labour force and travel, plus the largest wealth transfer in Australian history as roughly a trillion dollars moves out of super. Nick explains why frugal baby boomers are hard to convince to spend, the case for helping children into property now rather than later, and a super re-contribution strategy that can cut tens of thousands off a child's inheritance tax. They close on land lease retirement communities, estate and will-challenge risk, and getting a plan in place before retirement age.
