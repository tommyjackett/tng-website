EP 219

Building Passive Income through Property

Nick reads an AFR story about a couple sitting on 8 million in residential property, net 4 million after ten years, who realise it will not fund the 300,000 passive income they want. That prompts a residential versus commercial comparison: on a 1.5 million asset the commercial property runs about 39,000 cashflow positive while the residential runs roughly 14,000 in the red, a 53,000 gap that needs a 50 percent capital-growth premium just to break even. Jason and Marty cover negative gearing, the tenant paying commercial outgoings, and why retirees keep commercial but sell residential. They close on planning, expert advice and treating commercial as the quieter, cashflow-first route.
