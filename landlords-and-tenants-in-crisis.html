EP 202

Landlords and Tenants in Crisis

Marty brings the panic in the residential rental market to the table after rental stock in Victoria fell by more than 15,000 homes in a year. The three unpack why so many landlords are selling, walking through a couple who bought a 650,000 dollar rental in 2021 at 2.5 percent and went from a 5,106 dollar cashflow benefit to a 22,396 dollar annual loss at 6.8 percent. Jason and Nick explain the perfect storm of investors exiting while renters cannot find a place, why interest and insurance rather than land tax are the real cost, and how negative gearing and the tax refund keep some owners holding. They cover ways to ride it out, reviewing the mortgage rate, restructuring into tax-deductible investment debt, claiming depreciation and seeing an accountant. They close on when it genuinely makes sense to sell and getting a financial planner involved first.
