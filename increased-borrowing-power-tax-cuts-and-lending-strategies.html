EP 199

Increased Borrowing Power

Tax Cuts and Lending Strategies

Nick breaks down how to maximise borrowing power after APRA confirmed it is keeping the 3 percent serviceability buffer. The team explains what the buffer is and why it exists, then shows how the July 2024 stage 3 tax cuts leave more income to service debt, lifting a single borrower's capacity by about 27,000 and a couple's by about 75,000. They weigh the risk of repayments taking over half of household income, note that household savings have fallen to about 0.9 percent, and discuss where and what people can realistically afford to buy. Nick and Marty give their read on when interest rates might fall, and Nick finishes on non-bank lenders that assess income and expenses differently to lend more than the major banks.
