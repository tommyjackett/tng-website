EP 242

Enhanced Games - Can The Olympics Keep Up?

Nick raises the Enhanced Games, a competition that lets athletes use performance drugs, and the trio ask why Olympians earn so little despite the billions the Games generate. They walk through Paris Olympics revenue, the twenty thousand dollars Australia pays for a gold medal versus a million in Singapore, and the sponsorship gap between runners and swimmers. Marty weighs the ethics of altering body chemistry against backing equipment like super suits, while Jason argues the real problem is broken athlete pay that money will eventually disrupt. They land on the Olympics needing to reform incentives, from a players association to Drive to Survive style documentary revenue.
