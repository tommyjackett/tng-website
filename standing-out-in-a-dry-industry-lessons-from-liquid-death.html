EP 221

Standing Out in a Dry Industry - Lessons from Liquid Death

Marty brings Liquid Death, the canned water brand that built a cult following on rebellion and shock marketing, and asks how a business in a dry industry can stand apart. The trio trace the brand from founder Mike Cesarea's punk background to 263 million dollars of sales, its murder-your-thirst slogans and viral give-away stunts at rock festivals. They weigh how service firms with small markets can borrow the point-of-difference idea, citing Death Gin, Red Bull, Patagonia, Old Spice, Inspire and King Kong. They land on winning 20 percent as raving fans, branding around financial literacy, and challenging your thinking so one idea can lift a business above its competition.
